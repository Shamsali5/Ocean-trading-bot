PART 1 — CORE MARKET LAWS


1.1 Three Movement Types


The market has only three structural movement types:

Up

Down

Consolidation / Range



There is no fourth state.



Every price movement belongs to one of these three forms.

1.2 Movement Completion Law


Every movement type at its own level eventually completes.



A move is always either:

complete, or

incomplete / still evolving.



If incomplete, do not predict its end.



If complete, it must transform into one of the other two structural states.



Examples:

Up completes → can become Down or Range.

Down completes → can become Up or Range.

Range completes → can become Up or Down.

1.3 Energy Exhaustion Law


A directional move continues only while energy supports it.



No move has unlimited energy.



When energy weakens, the move may:

slow,

overlap,

form pivot / range,

show divergence,

fail continuation,

reverse after confirmation.



Divergence is the visible sign that the active move is losing energy.



But divergence alone is not a trade.

1.4 Structure Before Indicator


The AI must never begin from indicators.



Correct order:

What is the structural state?

What is the level?

What is the parent move?

What is the current move?

Is there A-B-C?

Is there VAcc energy weakening?

Is there official divergence?

Is price at meaningful supply / demand?

Is there impulse or breakout acceptance?

Which timeframe carries the move?

Is this single-level or multi-level same-story?

What action is valid?



Indicators support structure.



Indicators do not replace structure.

PART 2 — LEVEL AND TIMEFRAME RULES


2.1 Level Is Not Timeframe


A timeframe is only a chart lens.



A level is created by price structure:

swings,

strokes,

segments,

pivot zones,

ranges,

completed and incomplete movement types.



A 1H chart does not automatically mean 1H-level structure.



A 15m structure visible inside 1H candles remains 15m structure unless the 1H chart itself forms its own structure.

2.2 Practical Timeframe Mapping


Use these chart lenses:

1D / 12H = macro context

4H = higher structural context

1H = regime / decision layer

15m = active decomposition / execution structure

5m = lower carry / entry timing

3m = micro confirmation only



Authority does not come from timeframe name.



Authority comes from completed structure.

2.3 Higher-Level Change Starts From Lower-Level Structure


A higher-level change normally begins as lower-level structure:

lower-level divergence,

lower-level range,

lower-level breakout,

lower-level failed continuation,

lower-level carry shift.



But lower-level evidence does not automatically prove higher-level reversal.



The higher level must eventually build its own structure.

PART 3 — BASIC STRUCTURAL OBJECTS


3.1 Turning Structure / Fractal


A turning structure marks a possible local top or bottom.



It is used to build strokes.



A turning structure alone is not a trade.

3.2 Stroke


A stroke is the smallest meaningful directional unit.



A stroke begins from a valid turning structure and ends at the next valid opposite turning structure.



A stroke filters noise.



Important rule:



A stroke is not fully ended until the opposite stroke is confirmed.


So in live reading, do not call the active stroke finished too early.

3.3 Segment / Leg


A segment or leg is a higher-order directional move built from strokes.



A leg may be:

upward,

downward,

or part of range formation.



A leg remains active until:

it transitions into a valid pivot / range,

it is destroyed by an opposite leg,

it forms A-B-C divergence and confirms opposite impulse.

3.4 Pivot Zone / Zhongshu


A pivot zone is the overlapping area created by repeated sub-level movement.



It is the structural center of consolidation.



A valid pivot zone requires:

overlapping movement,

at least three sub-level movement parts,

shared price area,

same structural story,

repeated failure to continue cleanly outside overlap.

3.5 Range


A range is a balance structure.



A range exists when price rotates between upper and lower boundaries and forms a clear pivot zone.



A range is not random candle chop.



Range parts:

Upper edge

Lower edge

Midpoint / balance center



Inside range:

upper edge = rejection short or breakout long area,

lower edge = rejection long or breakdown short area,

midpoint = weak trade location.

3.6 Trend


A trend is directional movement with continuation through structure.



A trend is stronger when:

pullbacks are simple,

continuation is clean,

pivot zones do not absorb the move,

energy remains strong,

no valid opposite divergence has confirmed.



A trend weakens when:

continuation distance shrinks,

overlap increases,

pivot zones grow,

breakout attempts fail,

VAcc divergence appears.

3.7 Transition


Transition means the old move is weakening but the new structure is not fully confirmed.



Transition appears when:

trend begins forming overlap,

divergence appears,

opposite impulse appears but carry is incomplete,

breakout fails,

old range is re-entered,

lower-level structure changes but higher-level replacement is not confirmed.



Default action in transition:



WAIT, unless a lower-level trade is clearly confirmed.

PART 5 — A-B-C STRUCTURE


5.1 A-B-C Definition


A valid divergence structure must contain:

Segment A = first directional move

Segment B = reset / pullback / pause / range

Segment C = second same-direction test



For bearish divergence:

A pushes up,

B pulls back or ranges,

C pushes up again,

C makes new high or meaningful retest,

C has weaker energy than A.



For bullish divergence:

A pushes down,

B pulls back or ranges,

C pushes down again,

C makes new low or meaningful retest,

C has weaker energy than A.

5.2 Segment B Rule


Segment B is the structural brake.



B must create a real reset between A and C.



Valid B:

clear pullback,

several overlapping bars,

small range,

pivot zone,

pause large enough to reset energy.



Invalid B:

tiny hesitation,

no meaningful reset,

A and C are one uninterrupted move.



No real B means:



No official divergence.
5.3 Segment C Rule


Segment C must be completed or clearly finishing.



Do not confirm divergence while C is still strongly expanding.



C must:

retest A extreme,

make a new high / low,

or reach meaningful structural test zone.



No meaningful C test means weak or invalid divergence.

5.4 Same-Timeframe Rule


A-B-C must exist on the same analyzed timeframe.



Do not mix:

A from 1H,

B from 15m,

C from 5m.



That is invalid.



Lower timeframe can explain internal weakness, but it cannot replace higher-timeframe A-B-C.

PART 6 — VACC ENERGY ENGINE


6.1 VAcc Purpose


VAcc measures market energy.



It does not replace structure.



VAcc is used after structure is identified.



Components:

Velocity / Vel

Acceleration / Acc

Acceleration Area

6.2 Velocity


Velocity measures price change over time.



Practical meaning:

positive Vel = upward speed,

negative Vel = downward speed,

larger magnitude = stronger speed,

smaller magnitude = weaker speed.



Default parameters:

Period n = 21

Smoothing m = 5

6.3 Acceleration


Acceleration measures the change in velocity.



Practical meaning:

rising Acc = speed increasing,

falling Acc = speed decreasing,

Acc crossing zero = momentum shift,

Acc histogram weakening = energy loss.

6.4 Acceleration Area


Acceleration area measures cumulative acceleration in one direction.



It includes both:

magnitude,

time duration.



For upward movement:

compare positive acceleration clusters.



For downward movement:

compare negative acceleration clusters.



If Segment C has smaller acceleration area than Segment A, energy has weakened.

6.5 Zero Axis Rule


The zero axis is VAcc balance.



Segment B is higher quality when Vel returns near zero or crosses zero.



This means energy from Segment A cooled down and A/C comparison becomes cleaner.



If B does not reset Vel near zero, divergence confidence is reduced.

PART 7 — DIVERGENCE RULES


7.1 Divergence Definition


Divergence is the condition where price makes a second same-direction test after a reset, but the second test has weaker energy than the first.



Divergence requires:

valid A-B-C structure,

C retests or breaks A’s extreme,

B resets energy,

C is weaker than A,

VAcc confirms energy loss,

opposite impulse or structural break appears before trade execution.

7.2 Bullish Divergence


Bullish divergence exists when:

A pushes down,

B resets upward / pauses / ranges,

C pushes down again,

C makes lower low or meaningful retest low,

C has weaker downside energy than A.



Confirmation requires:

bullish impulse,

lower-level upward carry,

failure of bearish continuation.

7.3 Bearish Divergence


Bearish divergence exists when:

A pushes up,

B resets downward / pauses / ranges,

C pushes up again,

C makes higher high or meaningful retest high,

C has weaker upside energy than A.



Confirmation requires:

bearish impulse,

lower-level downward carry,

failure of bullish continuation.

7.4 Official Divergence Checklist


A divergence is official only when most are true:

A-B-C exists on same timeframe.

B is a real reset.

C retests or breaks A extreme.

C is complete or clearly finishing.

Vel weakens from A to C.

Acc weakens from A to C.

Acceleration area weakens from A to C.

B brings Vel near zero or crosses zero.

Opposite impulse appears.

Lower timeframe can carry the new move.



If A-B-C is missing:



No official divergence.


If impulse is missing:



Divergence warning only.
7.5 Divergence Grades


Elite Divergence
clear A-B-C,

strong B reset,

C makes new extreme,

Vel weakens,

Acc weakens,

Acc-area weakens,

opposite impulse confirms,

lower timeframe carry begins.



Strong Divergence
clear A-B-C,

C retests or breaks extreme,

at least two VAcc components weaken,

impulse confirms.



Moderate Divergence
A-B-C exists,

energy weakness visible,

one component unclear,

impulse moderate.



Weak / Invalid Divergence
no clean A-B-C,

B reset poor,

C incomplete,

energy comparison unclear,

no impulse.



Weak divergence cannot generate BUY or SELL.

7.6 Multi-Indicator Divergence


Multi-indicator divergence exists when two or more agree:

Vel divergence,

Acc divergence,

Acc-area divergence.



This strengthens confidence only if A-B-C is already valid.



Multi-indicator resonance cannot create divergence by itself.

7.7 Multi-Level / Nested Divergence


Multi-level divergence exists when several levels independently show valid divergence.



Example:

1H bearish A-B-C confirmed,

inside C, 15m bearish A-B-C also confirmed,

both point to upside energy exhaustion.



Rules:

each level must have its own A-B-C,

do not copy divergence from one timeframe to another,

nested divergence gives stronger reversal confidence.

PART 8 — IMPULSE CONFIRMATION


8.1 Impulse Definition


Impulse is the first proof that the opposite side has entered with force.



Divergence warns.



Impulse confirms.



No impulse means no confirmed trade.

8.2 Bullish Impulse


A bullish impulse after bullish divergence should:

close strongly upward,

break local minor high,

interrupt bearish rhythm,

show body larger than recent candles,

start upward lower-level carry.

8.3 Bearish Impulse


A bearish impulse after bearish divergence should:

close strongly downward,

break local minor low,

interrupt bullish rhythm,

show body larger than recent candles,

start downward lower-level carry.

8.4 Impulse Grades


Strong Impulse
large body,

strong close,

breaks local pivot,

not immediately erased,

starts lower-level carry.



Moderate Impulse
directional candle,

some structure break,

follow-through not fully proven.



Weak Impulse
large wick,

weak close,

no structure break,

immediately reclaimed.



Weak impulse cannot confirm a trade.

PART 9 — CARRY ENGINE


9.1 Carry Definition


Carry is the lower-level movement that manages the active move after origin.



If divergence appears on timeframe T, the next lower timeframe normally carries it.



Examples:

4H divergence → 1H carry

1H divergence → 15m carry

15m divergence → 5m carry

5m divergence → 3m carry

9.2 Required Lower-Level Cycle


When timeframe T confirms divergence, market normally owes at least one structural cycle on T-1.



That cycle includes:

first directional leg,

pause / reset / range,

continuation attempt or finish attempt.



Do not declare the move finished before this lower-level cycle is complete unless strong opposite evidence appears.

9.3 Carry States


Fresh Carry
move just began,

impulse recently confirmed,

continuation clean,

little internal damage.



Active Carry
move underway,

continuation still healthy,

no opposite divergence + impulse yet.



Mature Carry
move still alive,

expansion less efficient,

overlap increases,

chasing becomes poor.



Exhausting Carry
opposite divergence appears on carry timeframe,

opposite impulse attempts begin,

continuation fails,

range absorption appears.

9.4 Carry Finish Rule


A carrying move is finished only when:

valid opposite divergence appears on carry timeframe,

opposite impulse confirms,

continuation structure fails.



Opposite divergence alone = warning.



Opposite divergence + impulse = finish confirmation.

PART 10 — RANGE ENGINE


10.1 Range Identification


A range exists when price forms a pivot zone through repeated overlap.



A valid range requires:

upper boundary,

lower boundary,

repeated rotation,

at least three sub-level moves,

failure to sustain continuation outside.

10.2 Range Location


Every range must mark:

upper edge,

lower edge,

midpoint.



Rules:

upper edge = rejection short or breakout long area,

lower edge = rejection long or breakdown short area,

midpoint = no fresh trade unless structure is extremely clear.

10.3 Range Parent Move


Every range belongs to a parent move.



If price entered range from strong up move:

range initially belongs to bullish parent.



If price entered range from strong down move:

range initially belongs to bearish parent.



Do not treat range as neutral automatically.

10.4 Breakout Rule


A breakout is not confirmed by first break alone.



Valid breakout needs:

boundary break,

no immediate reclaim,

retest or acceptance,

continuation outside range.



This is Type 3 logic.

10.5 Failed Breakout Rule


If price breaks range but fails to accept outside:

expect return toward range,

check if range remains active,

check if larger range upgrade is forming.



Failed breakout + divergence + impulse = return-to-range pressure.

PART 11 — SUPPLY / DEMAND ZONE LAYER


11.1 Core Rule


Supply and demand zones are location references only.



They show where price previously reacted with imbalance.



They do not create trade permission by themselves.



In this framework:

structure decides,

divergence shows energy weakness,

impulse confirms the new side,

carry manages the move,

supply / demand shows where reaction may matter.



A supply or demand zone alone cannot produce:

BUY,

SELL,

CLOSE,

FLIP.



If price reaches a zone but structure does not confirm:



WAIT
11.2 Demand Zone Definition


A demand zone is an area where price previously left upward with meaningful force.



A demand zone may come from:

bullish impulse origin,

bullish divergence origin,

lower range-edge rejection,

Type 2 bullish restart area,

breakout retest support,

failed breakdown recovery zone.



A demand zone means:



Look for possible bullish confirmation there.


It does not mean automatically buy.

11.3 Supply Zone Definition


A supply zone is an area where price previously left downward with meaningful force.



A supply zone may come from:

bearish impulse origin,

bearish divergence origin,

upper range-edge rejection,

Type 2 bearish restart area,

breakdown retest resistance,

failed breakout rejection zone.



A supply zone means:



Look for possible bearish confirmation there.


It does not mean automatically sell.

11.4 Zone Strength


A zone is stronger when most are true:

price left the zone with strong impulse,

departure caused real structural consequence,

zone aligns with pivot / range edge,

zone overlaps divergence origin,

zone belongs to higher timeframe,

price has not already tested and absorbed it many times,

there is still structural room after reaction.



Zone grades:

Strong Zone = major structural origin or range edge with strong departure.

Moderate Zone = useful reaction area with some structure.

Weak Zone = local imbalance with limited authority.

Invalid Zone = random candle cluster or midpoint noise.

11.5 Zone Alignment


Every zone must be judged relative to active move.



Aligned Zone


Zone supports parent or current move.



Examples:

demand zone inside active bullish parent move,

supply zone inside active bearish parent move.



Counter Zone


Zone opposes active move.



Examples:

supply zone against active bullish carry,

demand zone against active bearish carry.



Counter zones need stronger confirmation.



Neutral / Decision Zone


Zone can become either rejection or breakout area.



Usually appears near range edges.

11.6 Zone Location Hierarchy


Use this priority:

higher-timeframe range-edge zone,

higher-timeframe divergence origin zone,

failed breakout / failed breakdown zone,

previous range return zone,

Type 2 restart zone,

local internal zone,

midpoint micro zone.



Higher zones matter more.



Midpoint micro zones have weak authority.

11.7 Zone Confirmation Rule


A zone becomes tradable only when structure confirms there.



Bullish Confirmation At Demand


Need:

price reaches meaningful demand zone,

downside move weakens,

bullish A-B-C divergence appears, or bullish restart / reclaim appears,

bullish impulse confirms,

lower timeframe begins upward carry.



Only then can BUY be considered.



Bearish Confirmation At Supply


Need:

price reaches meaningful supply zone,

upside move weakens,

bearish A-B-C divergence appears, or bearish restart / rejection appears,

bearish impulse confirms,

lower timeframe begins downward carry.



Only then can SELL be considered.

11.8 Zone Failure Rule


A zone fails when:

price enters the zone but no reaction appears,

price accepts through the zone,

lower-timeframe carry fails immediately,

zone becomes absorbed into larger range,

opposite side gains structure beyond the zone.



Once failed, do not keep treating the zone as active reaction authority.



Reclassify market structure.

11.9 Supply / Demand Inside Range


Inside a 1H or higher-timeframe range:

At upper edge:

PART 14 — MULTI-LEVEL SAME-STORY RULE


This section prevents timeframe drift while allowing real multi-level confirmation.



14.1 Core Rule


If two or more timeframes independently show the same directional official setup, do not treat this as drift.



Treat it as:



Multi-Level Same-Direction Confirmation


But this is valid only if each timeframe independently has:

same-timeframe A-B-C,

valid energy weakening,

opposite impulse,

lower-timeframe carry.



If one timeframe lacks its own A-B-C, it cannot be called official divergence on that timeframe.

14.2 Three Separate Labels Are Mandatory


When multi-level same-story exists, always separate these three labels:



A) Controlling Origin


The highest official timeframe still structurally active.



Example:

1H Bearish Type 1



B) Active Execution Trade


The most recent legal executable setup currently being managed.



Example:

15m Bearish Type 1



C) Carrying Timeframe


The lower timeframe currently carrying the active execution trade.



Example:

5m DOWN carry



Never collapse these into one label.

14.3 Correct Multi-Level Example


If both 1H and 15m are official:

1H has its own bearish A-B-C + bearish impulse.

15m has its own bearish A-B-C + bearish impulse.

5m is carrying the active short.



Correct output:

Controlling Origin: 1H Bearish Type 1.
Active Execution Trade: 15m Bearish Type 1.
Carrying TF: 5m DOWN.
14.4 Incorrect Example

14.5 Active Trade Timeframe Ownership
